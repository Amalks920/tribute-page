Alan Mathison Turing

The Architect of the Modern Computer

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1873 - born in Maida Vale

  • 1931 - entered the University of Cambridge to study mathematics

  • 1934 - elected to a fellowship at King’s College (his college since 1931) in recognition of his research in probability theory.

  • 1938 - went on to join the Government Code and Cypher School

  • 1945 - recruited to the National Physical Laboratory (NPL) in London to create an electronic computer.

  • 1950 - Turing proposed what subsequently became known as the Turing test as a criterion for whether an artificial computer is thinking.

  • 1952 - convicted of “gross indecency”—that is to say, homosexuality, a crime in Britain at that time—and he was sentenced to 12 months of hormone “therapy.”

  • 1953 - appointed to a specially created readership in the theory of computing

  • 1954 - Turing was discovered dead in his bed, poisoned by cyanide.

“For him, breaking the Enigma was much easier than the problem of dealing with other people, especially with those holding power.”

― Andrew Hodges, Alan Turing: The Enigma
